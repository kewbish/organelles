Eukaria City - a thriving conurbation

Eukaryotes are part of the domain Eukaryota, one of the three main domains of life. They differ from prokaryotes in several main ways, the most prominent of which is that eukaryotes have a nucleus and organelles. They can build uni- or multi-cellular organisms, and have a cytoskeleton.

Eukaria City operates on the philosophy of interconnectedness - our power plants (mitochrondia) supply our executives (nucleus) and workers (ribosomes) with the fuel to keep our economy (proteins) going and our city expanding (cell duplication).
